A few weeks ago I had an idea. What if I built a Telegram channel that automatically finds and posts the best deals on Amazon and Walmart — all day, every day — without me having to lift a finger once it was set up?

The concept was simple. A Python bot. A couple of APIs. A channel with a funny name. Deals flowing in automatically. Affiliate commissions building quietly in the background while I went about my day.

I even had the name figured out: Acute Deal Syndrome. Because let's be honest — if you're the kind of person who gets excited when a delivery truck turns onto your street, you already have it.

"The bot was planned. The brand was built. The profile picture was perfect. Then Amazon said: not so fast."

The Part Nobody Talks About

To run an automated deal bot you need access to Amazon's Product Advertising API. And to get that access, Amazon requires you to have already made qualifying sales through their affiliate program. It's a classic chicken-and-egg problem — you need the API to scale, but you need sales to get the API.

I applied. Got rejected. Applied again. Rejected again. Applied a third time. You can probably guess what happened.

So here I am — doing it the old fashioned way. Manually finding deals. Manually posting them. One by one. Trying to hit 10 qualifying sales so Amazon will finally say yes and let me build the thing I've been planning for months.

Why I'm Still Here

Because the idea is solid. Because the channel is real. Because every deal I post manually is one step closer to the bot taking over and doing it automatically at scale. And because honestly — the deals I'm finding are genuinely good, and people deserve to know about them.

Acute Deal Syndrome is a Telegram channel where I post the best deals I find on Amazon and Walmart. Right now it's me, manually, every day. Soon it'll be a bot doing it around the clock across multiple channels. But the deals are real either way.

"10 sales. That's all I need. Then the bot takes over and ADS becomes what it was always meant to be."

What Happens When We Get There

The bot goes live. Deals start posting automatically across the ADS channel and the ADS: Haul channel — which is dedicated entirely to Amazon Haul finds under $20. The kind of things you didn't know you needed until you see them at $6.99.

The bot will be smart about it too — boosting the right categories around holidays, hunting for glitches and clearance deals, posting digests of the best live deals every few hours so nobody misses anything good.
